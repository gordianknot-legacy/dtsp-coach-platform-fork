> Executive Summary
>
> The Direct-to-Teacher Support Program (DTSP) is a remote,
> high-frequency coaching intervention designedto improve Grade 2
> teacher practice andstudent Foundational Literacy andNumeracy (FLN)
> outcomes in Uttar Pradesh. The program operates on the core premise
> that structuredpedagogy materials already exist—Teacher Guides (TG),
> workbooks, weekly assessments, andTaalika competency trackers—but
> teacher understanding andclassroom implementation remain low despite
> annual training.
>
> DTSP addresses this gapthrough intensive remote coaching: 2 individual
> coaching calls per month, 1 monthly webinar, and1 quarterly
> Video-Based Assessment (VBA) per teacher. In Year 1, this translates
> to approximately 24 individual coaching touches, 12 webinars, and4
> VBAs per teacher annually.
>
> The program's theory of change is anchoredin four teacher
> capabilities: planning withTG, delivering lessons aligned todaily
> plans, assessing student learning, andremediating based on assessment
> evidence. All coaching interactions are explicitly tiedto the state's
> structuredpedagogy package rather than introducing parallel content.
>
> Current operational reality: Coaches operate across 18+ tools (Sheets,
> Drive, WhatsApp, Meet, Forms, Calendar, notes), resulting in 75
> actions andapproximately 195 minutes of operational work per teacher
> per monthly cycle. The primary pain points are scheduling/con
> rmations, post-call documentation, call tracking, and VBA data
> capture.

> 14\. Conclusion
>
> DTSP is a high-frequency, remote coaching intervention designedto
> bridge the gapbetween structuredpedagogy materials andteacher practice
> in UP Grade 2 classrooms. Its success depends on operational
> discipline (scheduling, documentation, VBA execution), quality
> maintenance (agenda adherence, action clarity, coaching not
> lecturing), andtechnology enablement (uni edplatform reducing tool
> fragmentation from 18 to 3).
>
> The program's core bet is that 2 quality coaching touches per month,
> anchoredexplicitly to the state's TG/workbooks/assessments, can
> improve teacher capability in planning, delivery, assessment, and
>
> remediation—andthereby drive measurable student FLN gains. Year 1
> operational targets are 90%+ coverage, 85%+ completion rate, and 100%
> post-call summary discipline across 25 coaches and900 teachers.
>
> Success will be measurednot only by operational KPIs (P1, P2, P3
> framework) but ultimately by student learning outcomes: increased
> above-looppro ciency, reducedbelow-loopstruggling students, and
> narrowing teacher assessment accuracy gaps. Platform e ciency gains
> (195 min → 60 min per teacher per cycle) are enablers, not ends —they
> exist to allow coaches to spendmore time in instructionally meaningful
> conversations andless time in tool-switching overhead.
